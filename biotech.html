Biotechnology Breaktrough Genetic Experiment

During our BioTech Breakthrough Genetics Experiment, we did three main steps: DNA Extraction, PCR Amplification, and Gel Electrophoresis. The goal of the experiment was to compare our DNA with our classmates DNA and see if people with the same nationality had similar DNA patterns. First, we used a cotton swab to collect cheek cells from inside our mouths. We then transferred the cells into a tube with a buffer solution using a pipette. After that, we put the tube in an incubator at 95°C for 10 minutes. This helped break open the cells so the DNA could be used in the experiment. This step is called DNA Extraction. Next, we took a small amount of the DNA and added it to another tube along with a primer and master mix. We gently mixed everything together and placed the tube into the PCR machine. PCR Amplification is used to make many copies of specific sections of DNA so they are easier to study and compare. The final step was Gel Electrophoresis. In this process, we placed samples of DNA into small wells in a gel. An electric current moved the DNA through the gel, separating the pieces based on their size. This created bands that allowed us to compare our DNA with our classmates DNA and look for similarities and differences. Using B-roll footage recorded by our group members during the experiment, we used Adobe Premiere to edit and create our final documentary project for the class.
